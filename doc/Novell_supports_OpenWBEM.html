LINDON, Utah, Oct. 11 /PRNewswire/ -- Vintela -- a leading provider of
innovative platform integration solutions for Unix and Windows, and original
author and primary maintainer of the OpenWBEM implementation of the WBEM
standard (openwbem.com) -- today announced the support of OpenWBEM by Novell.
Novell joins a growing list of vendors who are publicly supporting OpenWBEM
for its strength in building a flexible and extensible management foundation.
This commitment was demonstrated in the recent introduction of Novell's SUSE
LINUX Enterprise Server 9, which uses OpenWBEM and CIM as a key element of the
platform management capabilities.
    "OpenWBEM and CIM-based management provide significant flexibility and
functionality," said Eric Anderson, director of software engineering at
Novell.  "The advantages presented through OpenWBEM-both from a technical and
competitive viewpoint -- are the driving force behind Novell's support for
this open source standard."
    Vintela's OpenWBEM is an enterprise-grade, open-source implementation of
Web Based Enterprise Management (WBEM), written in C++, suitable for
commercial and non-commercial applications.  It provides a foundation for
development of management frameworks that overcome cross-platform barriers and
empower true interoperability.  Developers can use OpenWBEM as a management
agent to provide applications for configuration and change management, system
health monitoring, and enterprise-wide management functionality.
    "OpenWBEM is great technology that we are pleased to see being adopted
from all corners of the industry," said Matt Peterson, CTO at Vintela, Inc.
"With OpenWBEM as the WBEM implementation for Vintela Management
Extensions -- a product at the core of Microsoft's cross-platform management
initiatives -- we anticipate a rapid growth in the acceptance, proliferation,
and success of the standard.  It truly extends CIM to all platforms."
    Vintela Management Extensions (VMX) is one of the first commercial
products to include OpenWBEM.  VMX 1.0 shipped in early August 2004.  It is a
Microsoft Systems Management Server 2003 administration console snap-in and
client that solves multi-platform administration and management problems by
seamlessly integrating with SMS 2003 to natively extend management and
administration capabilities to non-Windows platforms.  VMX enables IT
administrators to use existing SMS Management Points and Distribution Points
to manage Unix, Linux, and Mac OS X systems from within the existing SMS
console and infrastructure.

    For more information on OpenWBEM visit http://www.openwbem.com
